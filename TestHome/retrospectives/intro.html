Retrospectives

At the end of every sprint we try to have a Sprint Retrospective. These meetings are chaired by the Scrum Master and in them we discuss how we felt the sprint went. The retrospectives allow us to quickly highlight any issues within the team - maybe certain scripts are constant stumbling blocks, extra training is needed in specific areas or CCP didn't supply us with the enough information regarding a feature in progress. After a few retrospectives you can notice patterns and formulate action points based off them.

And of course - we focus on good stuff too! From defect count to helping out a fellow tester on an awkward script - it's good to keep an eye on that which we are doing well. Every little observation helps, it offers an opportunity to draw a line under the sprint and say - good job.

We break the retrospectives down into 3 main areas of discussion:

External issues

  • Multiple clients had to be installed
  • CCP requested that we didn't start any tests regarding Agents until day 3
  • Germs - people keep getting ill
Good Practices

  • Sprint finished with 2 days to spare
  • All priority 1s done within a week
  • Grant figured out a quicker way to test capital ships
Team Concerns

  • Outposts wasn't started on the first day - as it should be
  • Career path scripts still overly time consuming
  • 3 duplicate defects went in

Once we have completed our retrospective, an email will be sent out containing the minutes and you can also find a break down of our past retrospectives on these pages - with the most recent sprint at the top in the left-hand menu.
